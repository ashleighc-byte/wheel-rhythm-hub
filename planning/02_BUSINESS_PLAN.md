## Executive Summary

Free Wheeler Bike League is a virtual indoor cycling programme designed for secondary school students. Born in the Waikato and operated by Sport Waikato, Free Wheeler reimagines school sport by removing the barriers that stop young people from being active — travel costs, fixed schedules, team selection pressure, and limited school resources.

Students ride on smart Wattbikes installed in their schools, race in the MyWhoosh virtual cycling platform, log their sessions through a custom web app, and compete on school and regional leaderboards. The programme requires zero teacher time beyond basic promotion, costs nothing for students, and runs entirely digitally.

The 2026 pilot (7 Waikato schools, Term 2) is proving the concept. If successful, Free Wheeler is positioned to scale across New Zealand's 385 secondary schools, then into Australia and internationally — potentially becoming the defining model for digitally-enabled, equity-focused school sport.

## 1. The Problem

### Why Students Disengage from Sport
- **34% of low-income families** in NZ cannot afford competitive sport fees and travel
- **Rural students** face 3x the travel costs of urban peers to access sport
- Traditional sport is built around **fixed schedules, team selection, and performance pressure** — formats that alienate the majority of students, not just the elite
- Youth preferences are shifting toward **flexible, self-directed activity** (running, skating, gaming) that they can do on their own terms
- Schools face rising **resource constraints** — finding coaches, organising transport, managing volunteers is becoming unsustainable

### The Equity Gap
The students who benefit most from physical activity are often the ones least served by traditional school sport. The dropout rate from organised school sport in NZ accelerates from age 12 onward, particularly among:
- Students from lower-income households
- Rural and provincial students
- Students who are not already competitive athletes
- Girls and students with low sporting confidence

### The Opportunity
There is a large, underserved population of secondary school students who would engage with physical activity if the format met them where they are — flexible, technology-native, social, and free from judgement.

## 2. The Solution

Free Wheeler Bike League is a virtual cycling league that:

| Feature | How it addresses the problem |
|---------|----------------------------|
| Installed at school | Zero travel required |
| No cost to participate | Eliminates financial barrier |
| No fixed schedule | Students ride when they want |
| No team selection | Every student can participate |
| Gamified with points, levels, challenges | Intrinsically motivating; feels like a game |
| School leaderboard + regional competition | Social connection and healthy competition |
| Digital-first delivery | Scales with minimal ongoing staff time |
| MyWhoosh virtual racing environment | Immersive, engaging, screen-native experience |

## 3. How It Works (Programme Model)

### For Schools
1. Sport Waikato installs 2 x Wattbike smart bikes
2. School signs a simple MOU (no cost, minimal obligations)
3. Students register online — parental consent collected digitally
4. Teachers share a registration QR code and make one announcement
5. From that point, the programme runs itself

### For Students
1. Tap a QR code (or NFC token) to start a session
2. Ride in MyWhoosh on the Wattbike
3. Log the session in the Free Wheeler app (screenshot, mood, reflection)
4. Earn points, level up, see yourself on the leaderboard
5. Complete seasonal challenges and compete against other schools

### Season Structure
- **8-week season** aligned to school terms
- **4 challenge types**: Sprints, Endurance, Hill Climbs, Distance
- Challenges can be completed and repeated throughout the season
- Points accumulate; school leaderboard updates in real time

## 5. Business Model

### Revenue Streams

| Revenue Stream | Description | Unit Economics | Timeline |
|---------------|-------------|----------------|---------|
| Equipment leasing | Wattbike smart bikes placed in schools at a subsidised or cost-recovery rate | NZD $2,000–4,000/school/year | Year 1+ |
| Software licensing (SaaS) | Per-school or per-student licence for the app platform | NZD $500–2,000/school/year | Year 2+ |
| Regional Sports Trust licensing | Sell the model to other NZ RSTs to operate in their region | NZD $15,000–30,000/RST/year | Year 2+ |
| National/International licensing | Licence to Sport Australia, UK Sport, etc. | TBD | Year 4+ |
| Sponsorship (naming rights, equipment) | Wattbike, cycling brands, health/insurance companies | NZD $20,000–100,000+/year | Year 1+ |
| Sport NZ / government grants | Programme development and equity-focused expansion | NZD $50,000–250,000/grant | Ongoing |
| Data/research partnerships | Anonymised participation data for health research institutions | TBD | Year 3+ |

### Cost Structure (Per School, Per Year)

| Cost Item | Estimated Cost (NZD) |
|-----------|---------------------|
| Wattbike depreciation/leasing (2 bikes) | $3,000–5,000 |
| App hosting and technology (Supabase, Lovable) | $500–1,500 |
| MyWhoosh licensing | $500–1,000 |
| School relationship management (staff time) | $1,000–2,000 |
| Technical support | $500 |
| **Total per school** | **~$5,500–10,000** |

### Break-Even Analysis
- At NZD $8,000 revenue per school and NZD $6,000 cost: **$2,000 surplus per school**
- Break-even at **10 schools** with current Sport Waikato overhead model
- Profitable at **25+ schools** without additional grant funding

## 6. Competitive Landscape

| Competitor | What They Do | How Free Wheeler is Different |
|------------|-------------|------------------------------|
| Zwift | Virtual cycling for adults | Not designed for schools; costly; requires high-spec hardware |
| MyWhoosh | Virtual cycling platform | Free Wheeler USES MyWhoosh as a platform layer — not a competitor |
| Traditional school sport | Netball, football, athletics | Requires transport, fixed schedules, team selection |
| Fitness apps (Strava, Nike Run Club) | Individual tracking | Not designed for schools; no institutional support |
| SchoolSport NZ competitions | Traditional competition format | High admin burden; excludes many students |
| Peloton for Education | Emerging concept | US-focused; no NZ presence; expensive |

**Free Wheeler's Defensible Position:** We are the only programme combining smart bike hardware, gamified software, school-embedded delivery, and an equity-first philosophy in a single turnkey package for NZ schools.

## 10. Strategic Risks and Mitigations

| Risk | Mitigation |
|------|-----------|
| Schools not wanting to pay post-pilot | Prove ROI through data; seek continuation funding from Sport NZ |
| Technology becomes a barrier at scale | Invest in tech robustness; hire FTE developer |
| Wattbike partnership changes | Diversify equipment suppliers; explore Stages, Technogym |
| Competing programme launched | First-mover advantage; deepen school relationships; IP protection |
| Key person dependency (Grant) | Document all processes; build team redundancy |
| Grant funding dries up | Accelerate path to earned revenue; diversify funders |
