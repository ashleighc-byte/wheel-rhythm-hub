## Marketing Vision

Free Wheeler does not feel like a school programme — it feels like a game. Our marketing reflects this: high energy, visually compelling, student-first language, and a clear message that this is different from anything else at school.

**Tagline:** *Ride. Earn. Level Up.*
**Secondary tagline:** *No tryouts. No timetable. Just ride.*

## 4. Brand Guidelines

### Visual Identity

| Element | Specification |
|---------|--------------|
| Primary colour | Deep teal #0F5E6A |
| Secondary colour | Energetic amber #F5A623 |
| Accent | Electric green #39FF14 (leaderboard, gamification) |
| Typography | Heading: Montserrat ExtraBold / Body: Inter Regular |
| Tone of voice | Bold, inclusive, energetic — never corporate, never preachy |
| Imagery style | Action shots, low-angle, motion blur — dynamic not static |
| Logo usage | Always include "Free Wheeler" wordmark in full — no abbreviations |

### Copy Principles
- Lead with the student experience, not the sport
- Use second person: "You" not "Students"
- Avoid: "physical activity", "wellbeing programme", "participation"
- Use instead: "ride", "earn", "level up", "challenge", "beat your school"
- Reference the gamification at every touchpoint

## 5. Sponsorship and Partnership Marketing

### Naming Rights Opportunity
- "The [Brand] Free Wheeler Bike League" — primary sponsorship
- Brand on all in-school materials, social media, app interface, press releases
- Estimated value: NZD $20,000–$50,000/year

### Equipment Partnership — Wattbike
- Co-branded materials at every school
- Wattbike featured in all social content
- Case study to be published on Wattbike's website and ours
