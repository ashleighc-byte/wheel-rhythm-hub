## Purpose

This framework defines how Free Wheeler measures success during the Term 2 2026 pilot and beyond. It provides the evidence base for:
1. Programme improvement during the pilot
2. Scale-up decisions at the end of Term 2
3. Grant reporting to Sport NZ and other funders
4. Academic research partnerships in Year 2+
5. National and international licensing conversations

---

## 1. Evaluation Questions

The pilot is designed to answer these primary questions:

| # | Evaluation Question | Primary Evidence Source |
|---|---------------------|------------------------|
| EQ1 | Does Free Wheeler increase physical activity levels among participating students? | Session logs, Pre/Post survey Q3 |
| EQ2 | Does the programme reach students who are not already engaged in school sport? | Pre survey Q5 (prior sport participation) |
| EQ3 | Is the programme equitable — does it attract students from diverse backgrounds? | Registration demographics, school decile data |
| EQ4 | Is the programme sustainable without significant teacher input? | Teacher survey Q3 (admin burden rating) |
| EQ5 | Do students enjoy the experience and want to continue? | Student NPS, Post Phase Q10 (continue?) |
| EQ6 | Is the technology fit for purpose? | App issue log, teacher survey Q3, dropout rate |
| EQ7 | What is the cost-effectiveness of the programme? | Cost per student per session |

## 3. Survey Instrument

### Pre Phase Survey (Baseline)

| Q# | Question | Format | Evaluation Use |
|----|---------|--------|----------------|
| Q1 | How often do you exercise outside of school? | 1–5 scale (Never to Daily) | Baseline activity level |
| Q2 | How often do you participate in school sport? | 1–5 scale | School sport baseline |
| Q3 | How much do you enjoy being physically active? | 1–5 scale | Enjoyment baseline |
| Q4 | How confident do you feel trying a new sport or activity? | 1–5 scale | Confidence baseline |
| Q5 | Do you currently play any sport in a team or competition? | Yes / No | EQ2 non-sport players |
| Q6 | What stops you from being more active? | Multi-choice (cost, time, transport, injury, don't enjoy, no opportunity, nothing stops me) | Barrier mapping |
| Q7 | Have you used a smart exercise bike before? (e.g. Peloton, Wattbike) | Yes / No | Tech familiarity |
| Q8 | What do you think about the Free Wheeler programme so far? | Open text | Baseline sentiment |

### Mid Phase Survey (Check-In)

| Q# | Question | Format | Evaluation Use |
|----|---------|--------|----------------|
| Q1 | How many times have you ridden so far this season? | Number | Self-reported sessions |
| Q2 | How much are you enjoying Free Wheeler? | 1–5 scale | Mid-point enjoyment |
| Q3 | What's the BEST thing about Free Wheeler? | Open text | Qualitative highlight |
| Q4 | What's the WORST thing about Free Wheeler? | Open text | Qualitative barrier |
| Q5 | Has anything stopped you from riding more? | Multi-choice | Barrier tracking |
| Q6 | Is there anything you'd change about the programme? | Open text | Improvement input |

### Post Phase Survey (Outcomes)

| Q# | Question | Format | Evaluation Use |
|----|---------|--------|----------------|
| Q1 | How many times did you ride during this season? | Number | Self-reported total |
| Q2 | How often do you now exercise outside of school? | 1–5 scale | Post-activity level (compare to Pre Q1) |
| Q3 | How much do you now enjoy being physically active? | 1–5 scale | Post-enjoyment (compare to Pre Q3) |
| Q4 | How confident do you feel trying a new sport or activity? | 1–5 scale | Post-confidence (compare to Pre Q4) |
| Q5 | Did Free Wheeler make you more active than you would have been otherwise? | Yes / Somewhat / No | Attribution |
| Q6 | Did the points and leaderboard motivate you to ride more? | 1–5 scale | Gamification effectiveness |
| Q7 | Did the challenges (Sprint, Endurance, etc.) motivate you? | 1–5 scale | Challenge design |
| Q8 | Would you recommend Free Wheeler to a friend? | Yes / Maybe / No | Word-of-mouth indicator |
| Q9 | On a scale of 0–10, how likely are you to recommend Free Wheeler to a friend? | 0–10 scale | NPS calculation |
| Q10 | Would you want to do Free Wheeler again next term? | Definitely / Probably / Unsure / No | Continuation intent |
| Q11 | What was the best part of Free Wheeler? | Open text | Qualitative outcome |
| Q12 | What would make Free Wheeler even better? | Open text | Improvement input |

### Net Promoter Score Calculation
- **Promoters**: Q9 score 9–10
- **Passives**: Q9 score 7–8
- **Detractors**: Q9 score 0–6
- **NPS = % Promoters – % Detractors**
- Target NPS: +30 or above (very strong for a school programme)

## 4. Data Analysis Plan

| Analysis | Method | Tool | Timing |
|----------|--------|------|--------|

## 6. Ethical Considerations

| Area | Approach |
|------|---------|
| Consent | Parental consent collected digitally before registration. Students can withdraw at any time. |
| Data privacy | No PII visible in public-facing leaderboards. Airtable access restricted to Sport Waikato staff. |
| Anonymisation | Research outputs use school-level and cohort-level data only — no individual students identified. |
| Wellbeing | Programme is voluntary; no penalty for non-participation. Survey questions reviewed for sensitivity. |
| Student quotes | Individual quotes in reports require additional consent from student (and parent if under 16). |

## 7. Scale-Up Evidence Requirements

To justify Phase 2 expansion, the following evidence thresholds must be met:

| Evidence | Threshold |
|---------|-----------|
| Participation data showing meaningful session volumes | 200+ sessions across 7 schools |
| Survey data showing positive wellbeing / enjoyment outcomes | Mean NPS ≥ 6.5, enjoyment ≥ 3.5/5 post |
| Evidence of reaching non-traditional sport participants | ≥40% of students not previously in school sport |
| School continuation intent | ≥5 of 7 schools wanting to continue |
| Low admin burden for schools | Teacher satisfaction ≥4/5 on admin ease |
| Cost-effectiveness case | Cost per engaged student <$750 |
