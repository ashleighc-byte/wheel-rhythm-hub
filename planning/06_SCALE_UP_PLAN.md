## Scale-Up Vision

Free Wheeler will grow from a 7-school Waikato pilot to a nationally recognised school sport programme embedded in every willing NZ secondary school — and then into Australia, the UK, and beyond. The model is designed to scale: once the technology, operations, and partnerships are proven, expansion is largely a logistics and relationship challenge, not a technology rebuild.

**Scale Vision by 2030:** 200+ NZ secondary schools, 2 Australian state pilots, evaluation published in international sport science journals, recognised by Sport NZ as a flagship equity programme.

## Phase 0 — Proof of Concept (Term 2 2026)

**Goal:** Prove the model works. Collect the data to justify expansion.

### Key Actions
- [ ] Run 8-week pilot across 7 Waikato schools
- [ ] Collect complete Pre, Mid, and Post Phase survey data
- [ ] Generate pilot report with KPIs vs targets
- [ ] Get 5 of 7 schools to express interest in continuing
- [ ] Document all operational learnings

### Success Criteria
| Metric | Threshold to Proceed |
|--------|---------------------|
| Registered students | 50+ |
| Total sessions logged | 200+ |
| Post Phase survey completion | 70%+ |
| Student NPS | 6.5+/10 |
| Schools wanting to continue | 5 of 7 |

## Phase 1 — Waikato Expansion (Term 3 2026 – End 2026)

**Goal:** Double school count within Waikato. Begin charging schools for continuation.

### Key Actions
- [ ] Re-enrol all 7 pilot schools for Term 3
- [ ] Recruit 8–18 new schools using pilot data as proof
- [ ] Introduce school fee structure (NZD $2,000–5,000/year equipment + platform)
- [ ] Apply for Sport NZ scale-up grant (target: $100,000)
- [ ] Hire part-time Programme Coordinator (20 hrs/week)
- [ ] Build second cohort of bikes (additional 20–36 Wattbikes required)
- [ ] Launch teacher dashboard with basic reporting
- [ ] Formalise Wattbike partnership agreement for ongoing supply

### Funding Requirement
- Additional equipment: ~NZD $60,000 (20 bikes × $3,000)
- Programme Coordinator salary (part-time): ~NZD $25,000
- Tech development: ~NZD $15,000
- **Total Phase 1 budget**: ~NZD $100,000

### School Recruitment Funnel (Phase 1)
1. Send pilot results to all Waikato secondary school principals not in pilot (target: 30 schools)
2. Phone follow-up to top 20 prospects (prioritise decile 1–4)
3. School visits for interested principals (target: 15 schools)
4. Convert 8–18 new schools
5. Re-enrol existing 7 pilot schools

## Phase 2 — North Island RST Network (2027)

**Goal:** Extend the model to Auckland, Bay of Plenty, and surrounding RSTs through a licensing arrangement.

### Key Actions
- [ ] Present Free Wheeler model at RST National Hui (Feb 2027)
- [ ] Identify 3 partner RSTs for licensing in 2027
- [ ] Develop RST Operations Manual (how to run Free Wheeler in your region)
- [ ] Build RST admin portal (each RST sees their region's schools only)
- [ ] Negotiate equipment supply agreement with Wattbike for national pricing
- [ ] Engage Wattbike for national partnership (equipment sponsor / co-investor)
- [ ] Hire full-time Programme Coordinator
- [ ] Target 60 schools across 4 RST regions by end of 2027

### RST Licensing Model
Each partner RST pays a licensing fee to Sport Waikato to operate the programme in their region:

| Component | Annual Fee (NZD) |
|-----------|-----------------|
| RST Licence (software + operations playbook) | $15,000–$25,000 |
| Per-school platform fee (charged by RST to school) | $500–$1,500 |
| Equipment procurement (passed through at cost) | At cost |

### Technology Requirements for Phase 2
- Multi-region dashboard (admin sees only their RST's schools)
- Automated reporting per RST region
- Bulk student import / school onboarding flow
- API integration with MyWhoosh for auto session sync
- See `10_TECH_ROADMAP.md` for full list

## Phase 3 — National Rollout (2028)

**Goal:** Free Wheeler active in all 16 NZ Regional Sports Trust regions. 120 schools.

### Key Actions
- [ ] All 16 RSTs signed as licensing partners (or directly managed by Sport Waikato)
- [ ] National equipment pool of 250+ Wattbikes
- [ ] National student database and leaderboard (all NZ students on single board)
- [ ] Annual national Free Wheeler championship event (in-person or virtual)
- [ ] Submit academic paper on programme outcomes to Journal of Sport Science (NZ)
- [ ] Engage Sport NZ as ongoing national partner ($250,000 annual grant target)
- [ ] Establish Free Wheeler as a named programme in the NZ School Sport calendar

### National Championship Concept
- End-of-year virtual event: Top 10 students from each RST region compete in a live race on MyWhoosh
- Streamed online; schools + families can watch
- Prize: Trip to Auckland for a Wattbike training day / national champion status

## Phase 5 — International Pilot (2030)

**Goal:** Launch in 2 Australian states and 1 UK pilot. Begin licensing internationally.

### Australian Entry Strategy
- Partner with state Active/Sport body (e.g. Sport and Recreation Victoria)
- Initial: 10 schools per state (Melbourne, Sydney initially)
- Equipment via Wattbike Australia (direct relationship — Wattbike is UK/AU brand)
- Use NZ pilot as proof; adapt app for AUD pricing and AU curriculum terminology

### UK Entry Strategy
- Partner with Youth Sport Trust or equivalent national body
- Engage Wattbike UK HQ (Wattbike is a British company — natural ally)
- Target: 10 secondary schools, 1 region (e.g. Greater Manchester, West Midlands)

### International Licensing Model
| Component | Annual Fee |
|-----------|-----------|
| Country Master Licence | NZD $50,000–$100,000 |
| Per-region sub-licence | NZD $15,000–$25,000 |
| Platform fee (per school) | NZD $500–$1,000 |

## Scale-Up Risk Register

| Risk | Likelihood | Impact | Mitigation |
|------|-----------|--------|-----------|
| RST partners don't see value | Medium | High | Lead with pilot data; offer 1-term trial at no cost |
| Wattbike supply/pricing changes | Low | High | Diversify to include Technogym/Stages Cycling |
| Technology doesn't scale reliably | Medium | High | Invest in tech infrastructure ahead of need |
| Key person dependency on Grant | Medium | High | Document everything; hire coordinator by Phase 1 |
| Schools not willing to pay | Medium | High | Anchor on grant co-funding; emphasise zero marginal cost to school |
| International regulatory barriers | Low | Medium | Engage local partners who know their regulatory environment |
